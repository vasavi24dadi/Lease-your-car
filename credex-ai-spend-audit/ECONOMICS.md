# Economics

If Credex deployed this tool tomorrow, here is the unit economic breakdown:

**Value of a Converted Lead (LTV)**:
Assuming a "converted lead" is a Series A startup purchasing discounted AI credits through Credex:
- Average Annual Spend on AI tools (Cursor, Copilot, APIs) for a 50-person team: ~$20,000.
- If Credex takes a 10% margin on the discounted credits: $2,000/year.
- Assuming an average 2-year lifespan: **LTV = $4,000**.

**CAC by Channel (from GTM plan)**:
- **Cold Outbound**: Free financially, but costs time. If 100 emails = 5 audits = 1 lead = 0.1 closed deals, and 100 emails take 2 hours of SDR time ($30/hr), the CAC is $60 / 0.1 = **$600**.
- **Show HN / Organic**: Truly $0 CAC, but highly variable and non-repeatable.

**Conversion Rates to Profitability**:
Let's assume our blended CAC is $500. To maintain a healthy LTV:CAC ratio of 3:1 or better, we need our funnel to look like this from organic/outbound channels:
- 1,000 Site Visitors
- 200 Audits Completed (20%)
- 40 Leads Captured (20% of audits)
- 10 Consultations Booked (25% of leads, primarily those with high savings)
- 2 Deals Closed (20% close rate on consultations)

With 2 deals yielding $8,000 in LTV, and the marketing cost being minimal, the tool is highly profitable.

**Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months**:
To reach $1M ARR, assuming our margin per closed deal is $2,000/year, we need 500 active customers.
If we maintain the funnel above (1,000 visitors = 2 customers), we need:
- 250,000 visitors over 18 months (~14,000/month).
- To achieve this, the tool MUST have a viral loop (the "Shareable Result URL"). Every 1 completed audit needs to generate 0.2 new visitors through Twitter/Slack sharing (a K-factor of 0.2) to compound our outbound efforts.
- We would also likely need to introduce paid acquisition once organic channels saturate, maintaining a CAC under $600.
